Welcome to My Blog

February 11, 2026 · 5 min read

Welcome to my corner of the internet. This is a space where I'll be sharing thoughts, experiments, and reflections on technology, creativity, and whatever else captures my attention. I've been thinking about starting a blog for a while now, and finally decided to just build it and see where it goes.

Where most of my writing happens

There's something about the permanence of the web that feels both exciting and terrifying. Unlike social media posts that get buried in feeds, a blog post sits there, waiting to be discovered. It's a different kind of publishing—more intentional, more thoughtful.

"A blog is a conversation with yourself that anyone can listen in on."

Why Now?

I've been collecting notes, screenshots, and half-formed ideas for years. They sit in scattered folders and apps, never quite making it out into the world. Starting this blog is an attempt to change that—to give these thoughts a place to live and breathe.

The web feels different now than it did a few years ago. More crowded, but somehow also more fragmented. Personal websites and blogs feel like a return to something more human, less algorithmic. A place where you can build something that's truly yours.

* * *

What to Expect

I'm not entirely sure what this blog will become. Maybe it'll be mostly technical writing. Maybe it'll be more personal. Probably it'll be both. The only rule I'm setting for myself is to write when I have something to say, not according to some arbitrary schedule.

"The best blogs are the ones where the author is thinking out loud, working through ideas in real-time. You can feel them discovering things as they write."
— Someone on the Internet

Some posts will be polished essays. Others might just be links and commentary. Some will include code, screenshots, or diagrams. I want this to be a flexible space that can adapt to whatever form the idea needs to take.

Topics I'm Interested In

Right now, I'm thinking a lot about web design, creative coding, and how we build tools for thinking. I'm fascinated by the intersection of technology and creativity—how constraints can unlock new possibilities, how the medium shapes the message.

Building for the web never gets old

I'm also interested in the more philosophical questions: What makes a good website? What's worth building? How do we create things that are both useful and beautiful? These are questions I'll probably be exploring here.

* * *

A Space to Think

More than anything, I want this blog to be a space for thinking. Not just sharing finished thoughts, but working through ideas, making connections, changing my mind. Writing forces you to clarify your thinking in ways that just keeping notes never does.

If you're reading this, thanks for being here. Feel free to reach out if something resonates with you. The best part of putting things on the internet is the unexpected conversations that follow.

Let's see where this goes.
